Top Destinations

Rome

Rome is a must-see destination and a common stop on many European travel itineraries. The city is an important center of culture and history in the world. The most significant Roman artifact can be found here (the Colosseum), The best cuisine in Italy (Rome is home to numerous Michelin-starred restaurants), some of Michelangelo's greatest creations (in the Sistine Chapel), and, of course, the heart of Catholicism (the Vatican).

Venice

Few places in the world are as wonderful as Venice. Its distinctiveness is partly due to the city's network of canals, which function as highways and transport buses and water taxis in addition to its famed gondola fleet. As a result, there is a noticeable commotion here that can startle some visitors. Away from the crowded Grand Canal and Piazza San Marco, look for the smaller streets and canals for a tranquil holiday in Venice.

Amalfi Coast

It takes some effort to get to the cliff-side Amalfi Coast, but vacationers say the trip is well worth it. 13 vibrant beach communities with individual histories may be found along the area's more than twenty miles of shoreline. The two most well-known, Positano and Amalfi, both have a large number of hotels and eateries. While here, go hiking, unwind on the beach, and indulge in as much food as you like (there are multiple Michelin-starred restaurants in this region)

Florence

Despite being the complete antithesis of small-town Italy, Florence has a feeling of it. Florence, which is located in the magnificent Tuscan valley, is surrounded by charming roads that lead to lovely piazzas of different sizes. As you take in the beauty of the city, be sure to visit the Piazza del Duomo, home of the magnificent church with the same name, and the charming Ponte Vecchio, which looks out over the Arno River, ideally after sunset.

Capri

Due to its stunning surroundings, this little island in southern Italy has drawn many notable people throughout the years, including emperors and Hollywood stars. The Faraglioni rock formations and the Blue Grotto, where sunlight transforms the sea into an unbelievable brilliant shade of blue, are two of Capri's most stunning sites that are best experienced by boat. More breathtaking vistas may be seen on property at Villa Jovis, which was formerly home to Roman emperor Tiberius, and the Gardens of Augustus. Additionally, travelers may discover upscale stores and eateries in the town center.

Popular Italian Dishes

Risotto

A popular northern Italian meal with countless preparation options is risotto. It is made using northern-style rice, such as the Arborio, Carnaroli, and Vialone kinds, and is carefully cooked in broth to create a creamy, cheese-rich dish.

Pizza

Speaking of Italian cuisine always brings up the topic of pizza, a dish that serves as both a national emblem and a global representation of Italy. UNESCO has designated pizza as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.Every pizza maker has their own techniques, and each of the flour, water, brewer's yeast, and salt pizzas can have a variety of toppings or fillings added to it.

Pasta

Pasta is another another emblem, a beloved global icon and national dish. Originating in Sicily, it quickly spread its production to other coastal areas with ports and favorable weather for the product's drying. Naples and Imperia are two of the most well-known cities. However, Rome developed into the spaghetti center, and it was here that "spaghetti alla carbonara" became famous. It was created practically by accident by a young Bologna cook who combined bacon with egg yolk, milk, cream, and cheese.

Gnocchi

Gnocchi are small rounds of potato dough. Each region has its own variation, but the most typical ingredients are cheese, spinach, eggs, and a variety of sauces.
